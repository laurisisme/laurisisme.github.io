01 / Threshold

The Boundary
I Stepped Into

I stepped into a city mid-disappearance. Bacheng was being redeveloped, and with it, memories were quietly erased under the label of “preservation.”

02 / Approach

Navigating
Between Worlds

We treated memory as material. Through fieldwork, interviews, photography, and sound recording, we documented lived experiences — then used generative AI not to invent history, but to reconstruct what had already vanished. Lego-ing Bacheng is an interactive website archive where lost structures are digitally rebuilt as modular “lego” pieces. Through AI-generated reconstructions layered onto present-day photos, the project reveals the tension between what remains and what is remembered.

Question 1

How does redevelopment reshape memory and what counts as “preservation”?

Question 2

Who to listen to for “preservation”? What is to be preserved and how to preserve it?

03 / Artifact

Built at the
Intersection

Digital Storytelling

Combining traditional documentation with storytelling and website building, 3D modeling, we created a multifaceted narrative experience for users to explore. Each click and movement becomes a way of remembering. Preservation here is not static, but participatory, built piece by piece.

Multimedia Layer & AI Use

We combined the memory of old resident here with AI-generated reconstructions (DeepSeek, Hailuo) to visualize memories ethically. These animations are layered onto present-day photos, revealing tension between what remains and what is remembered.

04 / Reflections

What Crossing
Taught Me

Preservation is not about correctness — it’s about participation. When memory is excluded, redevelopment becomes erasure.
Lego-ing reflect our journey of discovering and combine pieces of information through our random encouters. Like lego blocks, people can build whatever they want with these pieces - likewise, our project doesn't give any answer for Fangfake or the "right preservation" - it's up to people interpretation. "Lego-ing" also means "let it going", sometimes, to move to the next steps, we have to accept the fact that some things are gone forever.
05 / The Unknown

The Horizon
Shifts

I want to explore how digital tools can serve as ethical witnesses, preserving stories without freezing them in time.
