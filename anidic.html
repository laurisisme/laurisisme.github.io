01 / Threshold

The Boundary
I Stepped Into

I stepped into the gap between human curiosity and ecological responsibility. Wild animals trafficking are common, but the worst thing is consumers usually don't know that are endangering species. And not many people know where to report when they spot illegal wildlife trading.

02 / Approach

Navigating
Between Worlds

ANIDIC identifies 20 animal species and provides contextual information for trekkers. We combined field data, AI training, and business modeling to design a tool that informs without disturbing wildlife.

Identify and Searching Animal Information

To help people better differentiate endangered species, I developed a system that can easier scan and detect the animals. The information about it's current status and penalty for trading will be shown right after.

Ugent Reporting System

Immediately report to local authorities with pre-filled information and location data to ensure swift action against illegal wildlife trade.

03 / Artifact

Built at the
Intersection

AI-powered fall-detection system

To identify species from images, I trained a model using TensorFlow and Google Teachable Machine, achieving 85% accuracy across 10 endangered species.

User Targeting and Education

We first target trekkers who often interact with nature. By designing an interface system, it creates a fun space to user to discover and get to know more information about animals they meet, sending report, first step to build a more sustainable lifestyle.

04 / Reflections

What Crossing
Taught Me

Technology can foster respect for nature — when designed with restraint.
For me, biodiversity is about which way and what attitude should we use to treat other creatures. I believe ANIDIC was born to connect people closer to the nature.
